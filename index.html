What We Do

Hurricane Resiliency Pruning & Safe Removals

In Southeast Texas, high wind is a constant threat. We do not just hack branches off, we make precise, calculated weight-distribution cuts on live oaks, elms, and cypress to keep them sound and standing.

Canopy Thinning & Wind Resistance Reduction

By selectively removing high-resistance secondary limbs, we allow gale-force hurricane winds to blow through the canopy, reducing mechanical leverage and the risk of storm failure.

Low-Impact Surgical Removal

When a compromised tree must be cleared, we use state-of-the-art rigging systems and premium compact aerial lifts to safely lower structural wood sections. Zero impact to your home, pristine sod, or landscape masonry.

What We Do

Remediating Destructive Invasives & Bayou Erosion

Aggressive non-natives like Chinese tallow, chinaberry, Brazilian peppertree, and Chinese privet are overrunning Southeast Texas, walling off the bayous and turning prairie and bottomland forest into monoculture. Left alone, they crowd out every native and erode the banks.

Invasive Tree & Privet Removal

Tallow, privet, and their kind resprout from a bare-cut stump, so we pair removal with targeted stump treatment, then replant with deep-rooted natives that actually hold the soil. No broadcast spraying.

Native Micro-Prairie Re-wilding

Establish a beautiful, self-sustaining micro-prairie of Gulf Coast Native Grasses. These natural root sponges absorb thousands of gallons of runoff, mitigating localized flooding around your home.

Tree Health

Formosan Termites & Trees in Decline

Formosan subterranean termites (Coptotermes formosanus) hollow living trees from the inside. The canopy can look full and healthy while the trunk is failing. We inspect for the structural warning signs - fungal conks, hollow sound, weak codominant unions - before a storm finds them first.

Root Flare & Decay Assessment

We check the root flare and trunk for decay, girdling roots, and the fungal conks (like Ganoderma) that signal internal rot, so problems get caught while there are still options.

Conservation-Minded by Default

We favor the least-toxic effective approach and protect the spiders, snakes, opossums, and soil fungi that keep a yard's pests in check. Removal and chemicals are a last resort, not a default.
